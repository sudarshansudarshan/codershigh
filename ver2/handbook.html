The Codershigh Handbook

How we do things: session culture, participation norms, facilitator role, and what to expect.

Session culture

  • Questions first — we spend time understanding the problem before solving
  • No rush to closure — we stay with uncertainty
  • Clarity over jargon — simple language beats complex terminology
  • Collective reasoning — we think together, not in parallel silence

Participation norms

  • Naïve questions are welcome — there is no stupid question
  • Disagreement should clarify, not dominate
  • Slowness is a strength, not a weakness
  • The group moves at the speed of understanding
  • Listen actively — hearing others' thinking is as valuable as sharing your own

What participants gain

  • Better intuition about algorithms and mathematical thinking
  • Confidence in discussing difficult ideas
  • Comfort with uncertainty and open-ended exploration
  • A more durable relationship with problem solving

What facilitators do

  • Hold the space for discussion
  • Connect ideas across sessions
  • Sharpen the question, not deliver the answer
  • Help the group notice deeper structure
  • Ensure all voices are heard

What to expect

Expect sessions that may not "finish." Expect questions that take longer than answers. Expect discomfort when a problem is harder than it looks. That is the point — we learn more from staying with difficulty than from rushing to resolution.

What this handbook is not

This handbook is not a syllabus, not a curriculum, and not a list of topics to cover. It is a description of how we learn together — the culture, the norms, and the spirit of collective thinking.
