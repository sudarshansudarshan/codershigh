# Discussion Summaries : The Pandit and the Pigeonhole

## Summary - 1:

### The meeting focused on exploring mathematical concepts, particularly the number e, through intuitive problem-solving rather than formula application.

### Main Discussion Topics:
### 1. Revenue Optimization Problem (Bus Problem):
- A bus with 100 seats charging ₹1000 per passenger, with a ₹50 penalty per empty seat.
- Participants solved this using multiple approaches:
    - Mathematical polynomial approach: Created equation 6000X - 50X².
    - Empirical brute force: Testing all values from 0-100.
    - Smart partitioning method: Dividing the range and narrowing down.
- Found optimal solution: 60 passengers yielding maximum revenue of ₹180,000.
- Purpose was to demonstrate the need for polynomials in mathematics.

### 2. Compound Interest Problem:
- Explored how interest accumulates with different compounding frequencies (yearly, monthly, daily, hourly).
- Observed that as compounding frequency increases, the interest converges to a specific value.
- Key finding: The result relates to e (approximately 2.718).
- Discussion revealed pattern: when denominator and power are equal, accuracy increases with more digits.

### 3. Understanding e:
- Group discovered e appears in gradual/continuous processes.
- Formula pattern: (1 + 1/X)<sup>X</sup> converges to e as X increases.
- Noted precision relationship: more zeros in denominator = more accurate approximation.

### 4. Phone Pigeonhole Problem:
- Brief discussion of Cuckoo hashing concept for storing phone numbers.

### The meeting emphasized building intuition and first-principles thinking rather than applying memorized formulas, with participants working to understand why mathematical constants like e emerge naturally.

## Summary - 2:

### Key Points from today's meeting:

### 1. Revenue Optimization Problem (Bus Problem):
- Discussed a Delhi to Manali bus scenario with 100 seats at ₹1000 per passenger.
- If seats are empty, remaining passengers pay ₹50 extra per empty seat.
- **Solution:** Maximum revenue of ₹180,000 occurs at 60 passengers (40 empty seats).
- Solved using polynomial equation 6000X - 50X<sup>2</sup>.
- Explored both spreadsheet approach and mathematical graphing.

### 2. Polynomial Applications:
- The bus problem demonstrated practical need for polynomials without using calculus.
- Found optimal point by plotting parabolic curve.
- Discussed approximation methods (dividing range into bins 50-75 passengers showed highest revenue).

### 3. Compound Interest and e (Euler's Number):
- Explored why e (~2.718) appears in continuous compounding.
- Demonstrated that as compounding frequency increases (yearly -> monthly -> daily -> hourly -> per second), interest converges to a limit.
- Formula: (1 + 1/n)<sup>n</sup> converges to e as n approaches infinity.
- With 12% annual interest, regardless of compounding frequency (daily/hourly/minute), the effective rate converges to 12.74%.

### 4. Mathematical Concepts Discussed:
- Why irrational numbers like e and pi are used as approximations for complex polynomials.
- e represents the limiting value when gradual increases occur over extended periods.
- Practical computational advantages of using e instead of calculating infinite series.

### 5. Pigeonhole Problem (Cuckoo Hashing):
- Briefly discussed smarter phone storage approach using cards and swapping.

### The group emphasized intuition-building and first-principles thinking over direct formula application.
